About Us


Gender Based Violence are violent acts committed against women, men, boys or girls,
due to social norms about roles and behaviour of each gender (Nkoyane, 2022).


There are many forms of Gender Based Violence.
Gender Based Violence includes physical abuse, sexual violence, psychological abuse and economic abuse.
However, victims of this kind of violence are often reluctant to come forward about the abuse they have experienced from their partner(s).
Some victims do not come forward because they believe that no-one will believe them,
some do not come forward because they do not have the emotional or physical strength to do so.
Leaving the emotional wounds of Gender Based Violence untreated can fester and end up having life threating effects on the victim.


Gender Based Violence has been increasing in South Africa over the past 5 years, yet victims of abuse have not been getting help for their situation(s).
Action Society has sounded the alarm on the large number of the gender-based violence cases in the Western Cape (Thebus, 2022).
Over 400 cases were transferred to the Social Development Departments GBV Command Centre in the province (Thebus, 2022).
Nolutho Menta, a Fieldworker stated, many people need support, but do not know where to go and then keep it to themselves, which is not assisting the victim or society (Combrink, 2022).
Getting help is very important for victims of Gender Based Violence.
Getting help from the right people is also important as it can help the victim improve both their methal & physical health.

The purpose of Themba is to help connect victims of Gender Based Violence with government sponsored therapists,
that will help them recover emotionally and psychologically from the abuse they have experienced.
